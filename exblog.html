Top 10 motivational books and why should you read them




“In the case of good books, the point is not to see how many of them you can get through, but rather how many can get through to you.” – Mortimer J. Adler
Reading motivational books can really change our perspective in seeing ourselves and the world around us. A habit of reading a good motivational book as part of the morning routine can be really life-transforming.


“Today a reader, tomorrow a leader.” – Margaret Fuller

Motivation is the fuel that drives you to achieve your goal. But, it is said that “People often say that motivation doesn’t last. Well, neither does bathing – that’s why we recommend it daily.” Zig Ziglar

One must read books daily for our daily dose of good thoughts, that uplifts and inspires you to achieve your goals. It creates awareness, bringing forward a host of choices and ultimately guiding you to take positive action.
“If we encounter a man of rare intellect, we should ask him what books he reads.” – Ralph Waldo Emerson
Here are my list of top ten motivational books and they are in no particular ranking. These are equally good, considering a range of topics they cover.


1. The Seven Habits of Highly Effective people   

Author: Stephen R . Covey





It states the formula of being effective and habits one must have, to achieve astounding personal and professional success.
The 7 habits, that the author talks about are,: Being proactive, beginning with the end in mind, putting first things first, thinking win-win, seeking first to understand and then to be understood, synergize and sharpen the saw.

Some of the quotes from the book are:

“Sow a thought, reap an action; sow an action, reap a habit; sow a habit, reap a character; sow a character, reap a destiny.” 
“Habit is the intersection of knowledge (what to do), skill (how to do), and desire (want to do).”“Love is a verb. Love – the feeling – is the fruit of love the verb or our loving actions. So love her. Sacrifice. Listen to her. Empathize. Appreciate. Affirm her.” 

2. How to win friends and influence people

Author: Dale Carnegie


This book advises you and teaches you simple techniques for dealing with people, so that you may understand and get along with them. It teaches you to improve your conversation skills so as to avoid arguments and win people over. You can make friends easily and become a  people person. It equips you to handle life’s situations.

Some quotes from the book:


“Any fool can criticize, complain, and condemn—and most fools do. But it takes character and self-control to be understanding and forgiving.”

“Instead of condemning people, let’s try to understand them. Let’s try to figure out why they do what they do. That’s a lot more profitable and intriguing than criticism; and it breeds sympathy, tolerance, and kindness. “To know all is to forgive all.”
3. Think  and Grow rich


Author: Napolean Hill





The title of the book may lead you to believe that the book is only about making money, but that’s not true. It’s principles help people succeed at whatever task they embark upon. This book states 13 principles in the form of philosophy for personal achievement and professional success.

Some quotes from the book are-
“Every adversity, every failure, every heartbreak, carries with it the seed of an equal or greater benefit.” 
“The starting point of all achievement is DESIRE. Keep this constantly in mind. Weak desire brings weak results, just as a small fire makes a small amount of heat.”
 4.  The Power of Habit: Why we do what we do in life and business

Author: Charles Duhigg




The entire book is explained with the help of diagrams and images, to show why we get stuck doing things we do. This is called a habit loop where people have a cue, a routine, and a reward. That’s why habits are hard to break. This book stands out among all others in this genre because the methods taught to change habits and behavior are backed by science and research.

Some quotes from the book are :

“The Golden Rule of Habit Change: You can't extinguish a bad habit, you can only change it.” 
“there’s nothing you can’t do if you get the habits right.”

5.  The monk who sold his Ferrari 

Author: Robin Sharma



It’s a book about passion, imagination, and dreaming. It’s a book to remind you of what it was like to be a child filled with limitless potential and wonder. This inspiring tale provides a step by step approach to living a life full of balance, abundance, joy, and courage.

Some inspiring quotes :

“Your "I CAN" is more important than your IQ.” 

“Investing in yourself is the best investment you will ever make. it will not only improve your life, but it will also improve the lives of all those around you.”


6. The power of Now 

Author: Echart Tolle




This book teaches enlightenment and spirituality through meditation and mindfulness technique that involves, letting go of our worries of past and future to only focussing on our present. This will separate us from the overthinking mind, thus removing negative influences of past and future and feeling of joy by focussing on the present.

Some quotes:

“Realize deeply that the present moment is all you have. Make the NOW the primary focus of your life.” 

“What a caterpillar calls the end of the world we call a butterfly.” 

“Nothing has happened in the past; it happened in the Now. Nothing will ever happen in the future; it will happen in the Now.”


7. See you at the Top 

Author: Zig Ziglar




This book was written to make people live a life of health, wealth, and happiness. He believes in living lives of principles. TO be successful and to reach the top, one must climb one step at a time in a certain order viz. Self-image, relationship with others, goals, attitude, work, and desire.

Some quotes :

"You don't have to be great to start, but you have to start to be great" 
“When obstacles arise, you change your direction to reach your goal; you do not change your decision to get there.”

8. The 80/20 principle 

Author: Richard Koch



The 80/20 principle says that the majority of our results and output come from minority of causes or inputs. An economist Vilfredo Pareto discovered this in 1897. Nowadays, it is called Pareto principle,  the Principle of Least Effort, the Principle of Imbalance, the Pareto Law, the Law of the Vital Few, and the Principle of Factor Sparsity. In reality, the ratio may not be 80:20. It could be 90:10 or 60:40.

Some quotes :

“The way to create something great is to create something simple.” 
“80 percent of products, or customers or employees, are only contributing 20 percent of profits; that there is great waste; that the most powerful resources of the company are being held back by a majority of much less effective resources; that profits could be multiplied if more of the best sort of products could be sold, employees hired, or customers attracted (or convinced to buy more from the firm).”

9. The 5Am club 

Author: Robin Sharma 



This book is a fantastic motivational book of the 21st century. It explains how freedom from distraction can help produce better results, slowing down our brain through hypofrontality is so important and how to create an effective 5 am morning routine, for a more productive day.

Some quotes :

“Take excellent care of the front end of your day, and the rest of your day will pretty much take care of itself. Own your morning. Elevate your life.” 
 “All change is hard at first, messy in the middle and gorgeous at the end.”“Dream big. Start small. Begin now”

10. The power of your subconscious mind

Author: Joseph Murphy 


   It is the perfect guide to unlocking the unlimitless potential that lies within each one of us.  It shows us how by changing our thoughts we can produce dramatic improvements in our life. He reveals the vast influence of the subconscious mind on all aspects of life, whether it is money, relationship, jobs, and happiness. It shows how to use our subconscious to achieve your goals and dreams.

Some quotes :

“Remember, you have the capacity to choose. Choose life! Choose love! Choose health!”\ 
“the law of the subconscious mind works for good and bad ideas alike. This law, when applied in a negative way, is the cause of failure, frustration, and unhappiness. However, when your habitual thinking is harmonious and constructive, you experience perfect health, success, and prosperity.”


Ok, guys, I have told you ten of my best  motivational books, which can drastically transform your 

life. I hope you liked it. Please leave a comment.
